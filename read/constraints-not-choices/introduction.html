A Note Before You Begin

This book does not argue that effort is meaningless. It argues that effort is not what sorts outcomes at scale. The distinction matters, but it is easy to miss.

Most of us carry a story about how the world works: work hard, make good choices, stay out of trouble, and you will be fine. If someone is not fine, the explanation seems obvious — they did not try hard enough, or they made the wrong choices. The system remains neutral. An impartial referee rewarding the diligent and penalizing the negligent.

This story feels fair because it is organized around something we can see. Effort is visible. Constraints are not.

What follows is not a moral argument. It does not accuse anyone of malice. It does not rely on ideology. It relies on structure — on what happens mechanically when survival requires clearing ongoing thresholds in systems with thin margins and ordinary risk.

The analysis is uncomfortable in places. That discomfort is not accidental. Systems built on constraint are genuinely difficult to see from the inside. This book is an attempt to look at them directly.

No villains are named here. No policy prescriptions are offered. The goal is not to tell you what to think, but to show how the world behaves when examined without moral shortcuts.
